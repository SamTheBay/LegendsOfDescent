Dungeon Game

Overview

So far the goal is to make a pretty strait forward fantasy RPG for the WP7 platform. One key piece is that we want to make the content of the game as programmatically dynamic as possible. This can manifest itself in being able to generate new dungeons, monsters, items and quests on the fly. The action in the game should be fast similar to that of Diablo of original Zelda. The graphics will be a 2d tile engine for the environment and 2d sprites for everything else (though we may want to put in some particle generators).

Gameplay Controls

  • Movement - If the player touches a point on the screen that does not have an enemy on it then they will move to that point
  • Attacking - If a player touches an enemy on the screen while they have a ranged weapon (or spell) equipped then they will fire in the direction of that enemy. If they do not have a ranged weapon they will move towards that enemy. If they are already next to the enemy and they have a melee weapon then they will attack.
  • Hold - There will be a button in the bottom left corner called the hold button. If the player is touching this button then it will prevent them from moving. Anywhere that they touch on the screen will result in them attacking in that direction instead of moving
  • Abilities - There will be about 6 - 10 slots somewhere on the screen where the user can attach fast actions. These can be spells, abilities, weapons, or consumable items. If the user taps a weapon or a ranged spell/ability then they will switch to that as their active attack. The next time they touch an enemy on the screen this is the attack that they will use. If they touch a consumable item they will use immediately. If they touch an "Instance use" spell or ability then it will be used at that moment. An example of this could be a super nova the hits everyone right around the player (thus the user doesn't need to select an enemy to use it on).
  • Life/Mana - On the side of the screen (left side?) there will be a vertical bar for life and verticle bar for health. If you double tap either of them it will automatically use the most appropriate potion for you.
  • Inventory - I am thinking there could be a stub on the side of the screen for the inventory. If you touch down on the stub and pan across the screen then it will pull open your inventory so that you can use it. You can close it in a similar fashion.
  • Pause - The hardware back button will cause the game to pause.
  • Minimap - Upper left corner? Transparent over the whole screen? Switch on and off via pause?

Player definition

Stats

  • There will be no concept of stats anymore. However, every level should increase your life, mana, damage

Abilities

  • Fighter - increases attack speed and damage with melee weapons as you upgrade it
  • Archer - increases attack speed and damage with bows as you upgrade it
  • Fireball - basic fire ball projectile. When upgrade it explodes and does aoe damage where it hits
  • Icebolt - basic ice ball projectile. When upgraded it slows enemies more for longer
  • Lightning - basic lightning projectile. Travels through enemies hitting all of them in a row instead of collisioning
  • Nova - lightning AOE damage around the user. Increase range / damage with levels
  • Chain Lightning - lightning the bounces from enemy to enemy. Upgrades make it do more bounces
  • Mind Blast - AOE stun enemies around you. Levels increase stun length and range
  • Inferno - Sustained attack fire spell. As long as you hold down the location you continue to spray fire damaging enemies in front of you over time
  • War Cry - AOE fear to enemies near you causing them to run away for a period of time. Levels increase range and length of fear
  • Stomp - jump up and down knocking back enemies around you and stunning for a short period
  • Multishot - shoot more than one arrow at once. increased levels increases the number of arrows you shoot
  • Teleport - teleport your character a distance in a direction. levels increase range

Dungeon Generation

Dungeons should be generated on the fly when the player reaches them. It would be good to have a couple different algorithms that will make dungeons with different feels. We can also get some different feels by having a few different tile sets that we can use.
